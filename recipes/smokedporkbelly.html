Smoked Pork Belly

When ever there is a family gathering I am asked to make my smoked pork belly. It's nothing fancy but it sure gets everyone talking and wanting more!

Ingredients

  • 1 slab of pork belly
  • 1 12 oz honey mustard (generic brand is fine)
  • 1 container of your favorite seasons
  • 1 18 oz container of your favorite bbq sauce (I use Sweet Baby Ray's)
  • 1 12 oz bag of brown sugar
  • 1 12 oz Frank's Red Hot sauce
  • Your favorite wood/pellets

Steps

  1. Pre-heat your smoker to 225 degrees F.
  2. Unpackage your pork belly and pat dry.
  3. Coat all sides of the pork belly with the honey mustard. This makes the dry seasoning stick to the meat.
  4. Evenly coat all sides of the pork belly with your seasoning. I use the green label Traeger seasoning.
  5. Add your favorite wood/pellets. I like to use apple with a little mesquite, or cherry.
  6. Place your pork belly on the top shelf with the fat side pointed down. You're going to leave the pork belly smoking for 3 hours.
  7. After 3 hours, remove the pork belly.
  8. Add the bbq sauce, brown sugar, and Frank's Red Hot sauce over the pork belly. No need mix the ingrediants, they will melt and mix in the heat.
  9. Cover and seal the pork belly and replace into the smoker for 2 hours.
  10. Remove the cover and place the pork belly back into the smoker for up to 1 more hour.
  11. Remove the container and cut the pork belly into cubes, roughly 2 inches, and serve!
